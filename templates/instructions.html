About Us

We are the Quarentined Crew and welcome to our application, Stocking. Stocking is an a real-time investment tool, specifically for the novice investor. This is not an application that you can actually invest through, but rather get more comfortable investing before actually doing so. Through Stocking, you can creae a mock portfolio of companies that you choose and see how they would have performed individually and collectively over a selected time period. We created this application to give new investors more confidence before entering the market by allowing them to see previous stock trends. We believe that personal wealth managment is something very important that everyone should practice and our hope is to expand the number of people that are comfortable investing.

Instructions

Begin by selecting the market history you would like to observe. You can choose between the previous five days, the last month, the last three months, previous year, or last five years. Next, enter in the amount of your initial deposit into the portfolio. Note that this is not any real money thay you have to deposit, Stocking is just an analytical and visualization tool, not a real way to invest. Entering in an initial amount will allow you to observe the resulting growth or shrink of your mock portfolio with a dollar amount instead of just a percentage. Next, enter in all of the company tickers that you want to see as part of your mock portfolio as well as the weights for each one. That weights are the percentage of your initial investment that will be invested into each of the provided companies. When you're ready to view your mock portfolio, click the "Run Analysis" button and scroll down where some visualizations should appear.

In the "Portfolio Performance" section you will be able to view how each company stock performed over the selected time period as well as the overall portfolio. You can drag a box over a certain area of the graph to zoom in on a certain time period. You can alco click on the stocks on the graph legend to toggle viewing them on the graph. There are also options to toggle some popular indexes to view i your mock portfolio is performing better or worse than general market trends. Below the graph are two pie charts. The first should show the percentage of each stock in the portfolio at the beginning of the time period. This will match the intial weights that you entered. The second will show the resulting contribution to your portfolio from each of the stocks at the end of the time period to allow you to see which companies are having the greatest significance to your portfolio.
